# Painted Wood

- **Visual Description:** opaque paint layer over subtle wood grain, with chipped edges exposing tan or brown fibers. Paint colors can be white, red, blue, green, or black.
- **Procedural Identity:** paint color layer, underlying directional grain, chip masks along scratches/edges, dirt in cracks.
- **Useful Variations:** like painted metal, but chips reveal fibrous matte wood instead of reflective metal.
- **Typical Channels:** non-metallic; roughness 0.55-0.95; chip height and grain normal.
